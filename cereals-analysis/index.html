Comparing Popular Breakfast Cereals

Introduction

Many people reach for cereal in the morning because it is so convenient, tasty, filling, and nutritious. Wait a minute, nutritious? Although cereals often market themselves as the perfect, quick, tasty, and healthy breakfast, I have often wondered how nutritious they really are. In this article, I compare 16 common breakfast cereals. Some of these cereals are popular because of their sweetness, so it is no surprise that they do not meet certain nutrition recommendations. Still, it is useful to see a comparison of nutrients in each of these cereals so that we can be more aware of what we eat. Note that a typical breakfast might include milk, fruit, and more, in addition to the cereal. The data below shows only the nutrients gained from 1 serving of the cereal, not including milk.

According to the U.S. FDA, certain nutrients should be consumed less, while others more. The nutrients to consume less of are Saturated Fat, Sodium, and Added Sugars. The nutrients to consume more of are Dietary Fiber, Vitamin D, Calcium, Iron, and Potassium. The % Daily Value on a label states how much of the recommended daily dosage of that nutrient is satisfied by eating one serving of the food. For nutrients for which low intake is recommended, the % daily value in one serving of food should be below 5%. For nutrients for which high intake is recommended, the % daily value should be above 20%.
